The
Danish Thesaurus

The Danish Thesaurus is a dictionary where concepts are grouped by meaning. It contains almost 200,000 words and expressions - of which over 112,000 are different - in 888 different sections and 22 chapters covering all topics from the universe to religion, illness and health, abstract concepts such as space, form and time, color and sound, emotions and thoughts, science and technology, politics and economics. 

It was first published in 2014 through support from the Carlsberg Foundation. Currently, we are working on an updated, online edition. I joined the project in May 2023.

The
Danish Dictionary

The Danish Dictionary is a corpus-based monolingual dictionary with more than 100,000 entries. The dictionary was first compiled in the period 1992-2003 and was first published in print from 2003-2005. It went online in 2009 at at ordnet.dk/ddo

The dictionary is continuously updated - usually about twice a year. I have since February 2023 assisted the work on expanding the dictionary with new lemmas (for instance, by developing a method to automatically detect previously overlooked mundane words).

The
COR-S
Project

A subpart of the COR project - A collaborative project between the Centre for Language Technology at UCPH, the Society for Danish Language and Literature (DSL), the Danish Language Council, and the Danish Agency for Digitisation.

The aim is to develop a Danish language resource of that contain the core information about the words (i.e., lemmas) of the language for AI purposes. 
The COR-S project focuses on making a coarse-grained sense inventory from current lexical resources (The Danish Dictionary and DanNet).

Master's Thesis
Project

An investigation of the optimal approach for creating Danish sense representations from a combination of word embedding models and handcrafted lexical resources. 

The aim of the sense representations is to use them for automatic sense tagging, which could enable other semantic tasks like sense clustering or sentiment analysis.
